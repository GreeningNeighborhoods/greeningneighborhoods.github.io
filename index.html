Goal: 1 gpf

If greater than 1 gpf click Low Flow and Instructions for a simple dual flush converter

How to find your gallons per minute

You'll need a mesuring cup and a way to keep time (Your phone probably has an app for that - You could just count in your head)

Now turn on your water and start the timer! Once you've filled for 10 seconds pull the measuring cup away and multiply the amount in your cup by 6 (10 seconds × 6 = 60 seconds or 1 minute)

Remember: You could fill for 2 seconds and multiply by 30 (2 × 30 = 60)

Keeping Score

Look at your family's water, electric and gas bills. Make note of the kilowatt hours of electricity, your therms (or CCF) of gas and your water use for the months before putting the energy saving measures in place. There should be a difference in the amount of energy and water you used and savings in your utility bills.

What Next?

Ultimately our goal should be to heat and cool our homes and drive our cars without depending on fossil fuels. The day will come when we will generate our electricity from the sunâ€™s energy and our battery powered cars will be charged with renewable fuels. We will then be living in what is being called net zero homes, meaning that we use more renewable energy sources than non-renewable sources.

In the meantime we can do something to become net zero. We can do with our homes what Google does, what Microsoft does, what Walmart does and what a number of cities like the District of Columbia and Houston Texas do. They become as energy efficient as possible, they generate some of their own power from wind, solar and geothermal and they purchase carbon offsets to make up for the non-renewable energy that they still use. Greening Neighborhoods is now offering carbon offsets as a means of making our homes net zero. We do this by offering carbon offsets through North American Wind. For every 1000 kWh per month purchased, individual homes will receive EPA guaranteed Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs) certifying that the home has offset their fossil fuel energy by the equivalent of 1,000 kWh of electricity or 125 therms of natural gas and saved 1,490 pounds of CO2 from being pumped into the atmosphere.
